How to Play Chess

Objective

Checkmate your opponent's king by attacking it in a way that it cannot escape capture.

Piece Movements

  • Pawn ♙: Moves forward 1 square (or 2 on first move), captures diagonally forward
  • Rook ♖: Moves any distance horizontally or vertically
  • Knight ♘: Moves in an L-shape (2 squares one direction, 1 perpendicular)
  • Bishop ♗: Moves any distance diagonally
  • Queen ♕: Moves any distance in any direction (combines rook + bishop)
  • King ♔: Moves 1 square in any direction

Capturing

Each piece (except the pawn) captures the same way it moves. Pawns capture diagonally. Remove captured pieces from the board.

Special Moves

  • Castling: Move your king 2 squares and the rook moves to the other side (special conditions apply)
  • En Passant: Pawn capture move that can happen when opponent moves their pawn 2 squares
  • Pawn Promotion: When a pawn reaches the opposite end, it becomes a queen, rook, bishop, or knight

Check and Checkmate

  • Check: The king is under direct attack
  • Checkmate: The king is in check and has no legal moves to escape
  • Stalemate: You're not in check but have no legal moves (draw)

Tips for Beginners

  • Control the center of the board
  • Develop your pieces early in the game
  • Protect your king with castling
  • Don't move the same piece twice without developing others
